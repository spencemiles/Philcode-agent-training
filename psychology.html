6 Principles of Persuasion

Reciprocity

The human tendency to return favors

  • Offer free value first (audit, mockup, advice)
  • "I noticed your site could improve X, here's a quick tip..."
  • Giving creates obligation to reciprocate

Example: "Let me create a free homepage mockup to show what's possible."

Scarcity

People want what's limited or exclusive

  • Highlight time-sensitive offers
  • Show what they're missing without a website
  • "Only 2 spots left this month"

Example: "Our design team has capacity for just 3 more clients this month."

Authority

People follow credible experts

  • Share credentials subtly ("We've helped 50+ businesses...")
  • Use testimonials & case studies
  • Dress professionally in meetings

Example: "Here's how we increased bookings for a salon like yours by 40%."

Consistency

People align with their commitments

  • Get small "yes" commitments first
  • "Do you agree that online presence matters today?"
  • Have them state their business goals

Example: "You mentioned wanting more customers - would a website help with that?"

Liking

People buy from those they like

  • Find common ground (location, interests)
  • Give genuine compliments
  • Mirror their communication style

Example: "I love how you've positioned your unique handmade products!"

Social Proof

People follow the crowd

  • Show similar businesses that succeeded
  • "Most shops in your area now have websites"
  • Use testimonials with names/photos

Example: "Here's what other Nairobi retailers say after getting their site..."

Client Personality Types

Tailor your approach based on their mindset

The Skeptic

  • Traits: Doubts need for website, questions ROI
  • Approach: Use data & case studies
  • Phrase: "What evidence would help you feel confident about this?"
  • Trigger: Authority + Social Proof

The Busy Owner

  • Traits: Overwhelmed, no time to think long-term
  • Approach: Simplify process, handle everything
  • Phrase: "We'll handle everything - you just approve the final design."
  • Trigger: Scarcity + Reciprocity

The Tech-Fearful

  • Traits: Anxious about technology
  • Approach: Reassure with training & support
  • Phrase: "We make it simple - you'll get easy-to-use tools and training."
  • Trigger: Liking + Authority

The Bargainer

  • Traits: Always wants discount, compares prices
  • Approach: Show premium value, then offer payment plan
  • Phrase: "This package gives you X value... We can split payments over 3 months."
  • Trigger: Scarcity + Consistency

Objection Handling Framework

Turn concerns into conversions

"I don't need a website - social media works fine."

Response: "You're right that social media works well now. But a website gives you full control, appears in Google searches, and works even if algorithms change. It's about owning your digital space while using social media to drive traffic there. Many of our clients use both to maximize results."

"It's too expensive."

Response: "I understand cost is important. Consider that most businesses earn back their website cost within [timeframe] through new customers. We offer payment plans, and remember - you're not just paying for a website, but for all the customers it will bring you 24/7."

"I'll think about it."

Response: "Of course! While you're thinking, may I ask what specific concerns you have? Often, we find clients are waiting because of X or Y - if we can address those now, you could start benefiting sooner."
